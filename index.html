Iceland

Iceland is a country of sharp contrasts. A place where fire and ice co-exist. Where dark winters are offset by the summer's midnight sun. A country where insular existence has spurred a rich and vibrant culture.

Things to do in Iceland

The Aurora Borealis, takes its name from the Roman goddess of dawn, Aurora, and the Greek name for the north wind, Boreas Fibenteribes to april, Iceland is a hotspot for this magnificent lightshow.

The Aurora Borealis, more commonly referred to as the Northern Lights is a natural phenomenon created when particles emitted by the sun interact with the atmosphere in the is magnetic field. This releases energy, causing peculiar luminous green streaks across Earth's the skies.


When making up to Iceland, it is hard not to pay special attention to the country's namesake namely, its 4.500 square miles of glacier, Ice climbing on the glaciers is practiced year-round and takes place mainly on the Sólheimajökull and Svinafellsjökull glaciers in the south of Iceland, to which day trips are offered from Reykjavik (and Skaftafell)
